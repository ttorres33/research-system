# From Offloading to Engagement: An Experimental Study on Structured Prompting

**Authors:** M Gerlich
**Published:** 2025
**Source:** [View Paper](https://example.com/paper1)

## Summary

### Introduction
- Studies the impact of generative AI on workplace decision-making
- Examines how prompt structure affects user engagement
- Focuses on knowledge work contexts

### Methodology
- Experimental design with 200 participants
- Compared structured vs unstructured prompting approaches
- Measured engagement levels and decision quality

### Key Findings
- Structured prompts led to 40% higher engagement
- Participants maintained critical thinking with guided prompts
- Unstructured use resulted in passive acceptance of AI outputs

### Implications
- Organizations should train employees on effective prompting
- Prompt design significantly impacts AI tool effectiveness
- Need for balance between automation and human judgment

### Limitations
- Limited to knowledge work context
- Short-term study (2 weeks)
- Self-reported engagement metrics
